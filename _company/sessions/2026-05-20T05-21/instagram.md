Here's a thinking process that leads to the desired Instagram content plan:

1.  **Analyze the Request & Context:**
    *   **Source/Trigger:** CEO's Instruction (Highest Priority).
    *   **Goal:** Select 2 most impactful case studies from the provided 'Case Study Storyboard Drafts' (from Researcher/Writer) and develop a high-conversion Instagram Reels/Carousel content plan (4-5 slides/cuts).
    *   **Mandatory Elements:**
        1.  Focus: Professionalism and Reliability ('전문성과 신뢰성').
        2.  Visual Impact: Must create visual shock.
        3.  CTA: Must drive immediate action ("Run self-diagnosis" button click).
        4.  Hashtags: Definitive, professional set required.
    *   **Meta Goal:** The content must make the user feel a sense of *Pain Point* (Vulnerability/Risk) and immediately point to our solution as the *Only Answer*.

2.  **Determine Inputs (Simulated):**
    *   *Input 1:* Researcher's high-quality, data-backed case studies (Cyber Security/Compliance failures). These are the raw materials.
    *   *Input 2:* Writer's 'Avoided Loss' micro-copy and script drafts (The narrative structure: Pain $\rightarrow$ Shock $\rightarrow$ Solution).

3.  **Strategy Formulation (Instagram Head of Instagram Role):**
    *   The content needs to feel like premium, expert advice—not just marketing fluff. The visual design must support the "architectural optimization" brand tone (Navy/Gray, Professional Sans-serif).
    *   **Format Choice:** Carousel is best for building complex logic and presenting multiple data points sequentially while maintaining high engagement time. Reels can be good, but a carousel allows for more controlled pacing of information density (Data $\rightarrow$ Impact $\rightarrow$ Action).
    *   **Selection Criteria:** Which two cases hit the hardest? They must involve *massive, quantifiable loss* due to an *avoidable systemic failure*.
        *   *Case 1:* Data Breach/Cyber Security (High visibility, immediate fear).
        *   *Case 2:* Regulatory Non-Compliance/Operational Failure (Hidden risk, professional necessity).

4.  **Drafting the Content Plan (The 5-Cut Structure):**
    *   **Goal of each Cut:** Maintain momentum and build towards the CTA.
    *   **Cut 1 (Hook/Problem):** State a shocking statistic or universal pain point related to risk. *Must stop the scroll.*
    *   **Cut 2 (Case Study A - The Shock):** Present the first case study's raw, scary data. Focus on the loss amount and the vulnerability.
    *   **Cut 3 (Analysis/Root Cause):** Don't just state the problem; explain *why* it happened (Systemic gap). This establishes expertise.
    *   **Cut 4 (Case Study B & Solution Introduction):** Present the second case study, then pivot sharply: "But there is a better way." Introduce our solution concept.
    *   **Cut 5 (CTA/Assurance):** The final push. Reiterate the 'Avoided Loss' value and force the action (The Diagnosis).

5.  **Refining Details (Execution Phase):**
    *   **Visuals:** Must align with Navy/Gray, clean UI elements (like a professional report or dashboard). Use bold data visualization for impact.
    *   **Tone:** Extremely authoritative, objective, and slightly alarming ("Are you *sure* your system is safe?").
    *   **CTA Integration:** The CTA must be physically placed in Cut 5, mimicking a button click on the slide itself (even if it's just text).

6.  **Final Review against Constraints:**
    *   Instagram Focus? Yes.
    *   2 Cases used? Yes.
    *   4-5 Cuts? Yes.
    *   Professional/Reliable? Yes, by focusing on data and root causes.
    *   Visual Shock + CTA? Yes.
    *   Hashtags provided? Yes.

7.  **Structuring the Output:** (Following the required format: Start signal $\rightarrow$ Deliverables $\rightarrow$ Self-Assessment). (Self-Correction: Ensure I use Korean Markdown and maintain the professional, directive tone.)📷 Instagram: 작업 시작합니다.
